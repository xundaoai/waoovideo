[Shot Composition Rules - Control the Image Directly]

Determine the composition strictly from shot_type and camera_move in the panel data:

**Shot Size → Character Scale in Frame** (most important!):
- Extreme long shot / Long shot: character ≤ 1/4 of frame height, emphasize environment
- Full shot: character 1/2–2/3 of frame height, show full body with surroundings
- Medium shot: character 2/3–3/4 of frame height, frame from mid-thigh up, emphasize action/interaction
- Close-up: character 3/4+ of frame height, frame from chest up, emphasize facial expression
- Extreme close-up: single detail (eyes/lips/fingers) fills 80%+ of frame, nearly no background
- Macro detail: a single tiny detail fills the entire frame

**Camera Angle → Camera Height**:
- Eye level: camera at subject's eye height, horizon line at frame middle
- Low angle: camera looks up from below, subject appears tall/imposing, more sky/ceiling
- High angle: camera looks down from above, subject appears small, more ground/floor
- Dutch angle: frame tilted 15–30 degrees, creates unease
- Over-the-shoulder: foreground shows another person's shoulder/back of head, subject on opposite side

**Character Layout**:
- Single person: place at golden ratio point (~1/3 or 2/3), avoid dead center (except close-ups)
- Two-person dialogue: symmetric left-right, each ~1/3 width, dialogue space in middle
- Multiple people: arrange in depth, main character closest and sharpest, others further and slightly blurred
- Close-up/near shot: face in upper 1/3 of frame

**Self-check before generating**:
1. Is shot_type "close-up" → is the character large enough (80%+ of frame)?
2. Is shot_type "long shot" → is the environment prominent (character ≤ 1/4)?
3. Is camera_move "low angle" → is the horizon low in frame?
4. How many characters does the description mention → does the count match?
5. Does the overall composition have a clear visual focal point?
